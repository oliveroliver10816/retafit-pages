Is the "Reta" Hype Real? A Plain-English Buyer's Guide

If your feed is full of "reta" posts, here is the short version. Reta is slang for retatrutide, a weight-loss drug that is still in clinical trials. It exists only as an injection, it is not approved, and you cannot legally buy it anywhere today.

That means every online shop selling "reta pills" as the drug is selling something unverified. Often those tablets contain nothing at all. That is the part the hype posts never mention.

What you CAN legally buy is a different category: natural, over-the-counter capsules that support metabolism, energy and weight management. They are supplements, not medicine, and honest brands say so directly. One example that states this clearly is the RetaFit official website — a vegan, caffeine-and-botanicals capsule with one-time pricing and a 30-day money-back guarantee.

Three simple checks before you buy anything in this category: the full ingredient list must be public, the payment must be one-time (no hidden subscription), and there must be a real refund window. Details like how to take it and what it costs should be answered openly — the RetaFit dosage FAQ is a good example of what transparent product information looks like.

Bottom line: the injection is not for sale, the "drug in a pill" offers are fake, and the legal supplement route is the only honest one on the market right now.

«Reta» i Norge: hva som er ekte, og hva som er tull
